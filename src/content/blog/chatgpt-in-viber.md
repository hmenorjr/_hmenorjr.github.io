The integration of ChatGPT into Viber marks a significant shift in how we interact with messaging apps, transforming them from simple communication tools into multifunctional platforms. While this move aligns with the industry trend of creating "super apps," it sparks a debate between enhanced utility and user autonomy.

## 🤔 The Case for Integration: Efficiency at Your Fingertips

Proponents of this integration argue that embedding AI directly into the chat interface offers unparalleled convenience. According to Rakuten Viber CEO Ofir Eyal, the goal is not to replace human judgment but to augment daily communication. The platform already offers features like **Link Summary** and **Chat Summary**, which allow users to quickly grasp the essence of long conversations or articles without leaving the app.

The ability to generate ideas, translate messages, and improve text quality within the same window where users talk to friends and family streamlines workflows. For a market like Bulgaria, where Viber reports high engagement with new features like Viber Pay, this integration is seen as a natural evolution toward a platform that combines communication, payments, and digital services. The underlying philosophy is that technology should serve people by making complex tasks simpler, provided the user retains control.

## 🕵️ The Controversy: Opt-Out Concerns and User Control

Despite the potential benefits, the rollout has faced criticism regarding user choice. Reports indicate that for some users, the ChatGPT integration appears as a separate chat or a tab that **cannot be deleted**, with no immediate option to opt out. This has led to backlash in communities concerned about the ubiquity of AI, with users expressing frustration over the inability to remove the feature if they prefer a traditional messaging experience.

Critics argue that while AI tools are powerful, they should remain optional add-ons rather than permanent fixtures in a personal communication space. The concern extends beyond mere interface clutter to broader questions about data privacy and the psychological impact of having an AI presence constantly available in private conversations. While Viber emphasizes its end-to-end encryption and commitment to security, the lack of an obvious "off switch" for the AI component remains a point of contention.

## ⚖️ The Verdict: A Balancing Act

The integration of ChatGPT into Viber represents a bold step toward the future of "super apps," where a single platform handles diverse aspects of digital life. However, its long-term success depends on striking a balance between innovation and user consent.

If Viber can address the concerns regarding mandatory presence and provide clear, easy-to-use controls for those who wish to disable AI features, it could set a standard for how messaging apps evolve. Until then, the feature remains a double-edged sword: a powerful tool for some, and an intrusive element for others. The ultimate test will be whether the platform can prove that this AI is truly a servant to the user, rather than an uninvited guest.
